# Industrial revenue gated by daily grid-supply ratio

Status: ready-for-agent
Category: enhancement

## Parent

[PRD: Economy & balance pass](../PRD.md)

## What to build

Make industrial tile revenue scale by the grid's daily met-demand fraction so that brownouts cost the industrial tile money — not just the city's happiness — and the industrialist is incentivized to keep power supply healthy. This is the only behavior change in this PRD that requires plumbing a new daily aggregate from `power.dispatch` into `pricing`.

Implementation:

- `power.dispatch` exposes a new daily aggregate: the hourly met-demand fraction averaged over the day (equivalently, the fraction of industrial demand actually served across the day).
- The industrial revenue helper in `world/pricing.py` gains a `power_supply_ratio` parameter (a value in `[0, 1]`) so gating is a pure-function transformation, not a side-effect at the call site.
- Daily industrial revenue in `world/sim.py` is computed using this aggregate.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] At `power_supply_ratio = 1.0`, industrial revenue equals today's workforce-only baseline.
- [ ] At `power_supply_ratio = 0.5`, industrial revenue is exactly halved.
- [ ] At `power_supply_ratio = 0.0`, industrial revenue is zero.
- [ ] The workforce-efficiency multiplier composes correctly with the supply-ratio multiplier (multiplicative, not additive).
- [ ] `power.dispatch` returns/exposes the daily met-demand fraction in a form sim can consume.
- [ ] New tests cover the three ratio points above and the workforce composition case.
- [ ] `make check` passes.

## Agent Brief

**Category:** enhancement
**Summary:** Scale industrial tile revenue by the day's met-demand fraction so brownouts cost the industrialist money — not just the city's happiness.

**Current behavior:**
An industrial tile draws 300 kW continuously, but its revenue is purely workforce-gated. During brownouts the city's happiness is punished while the industrial tile continues to print money — that reads wrong.

**Desired behavior:**
Industrial revenue scales by the grid's daily met-demand fraction (a value in `[0, 1]`, averaged over the day or equivalent to the fraction of industrial demand actually served across the day). At 1.0, revenue equals the workforce-only baseline. At 0.5, revenue is exactly halved. At 0.0, revenue is zero. The workforce-efficiency multiplier composes multiplicatively with the supply-ratio multiplier.

**Key interfaces:**
- A new daily aggregate exposed by the power dispatch module: the hourly met-demand fraction averaged across the day. Named so its widest meaning is clear (not "industrial supply ratio" — it's the grid's daily met-demand fraction, which industrial revenue happens to consume).
- The industrial revenue helper in the pricing module — gains a `power_supply_ratio: float` parameter. The function is pure: it transforms its inputs into a revenue figure with no side effects.
- The sim daily-revenue path for industrial tiles — passes the new aggregate into the pricing helper.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- [ ] `power_supply_ratio = 1.0` → industrial revenue equals today's workforce-only baseline.
- [ ] `power_supply_ratio = 0.5` → industrial revenue is exactly halved.
- [ ] `power_supply_ratio = 0.0` → industrial revenue is zero.
- [ ] Workforce-efficiency and supply-ratio compose multiplicatively (a 0.5 supply ratio and 0.8 workforce efficiency yield 0.4 × baseline).
- [ ] The new daily aggregate is exposed by dispatch in a form sim can consume directly.
- [ ] `make check` passes.

**Out of scope:**
- Routed/networked power (single-pool grid assumption stays).
- Commercial revenue (separate issue).
- Refinery throughput gating on grid supply (already exists via process-load coupling).
- Per-hour or per-event variable industrial revenue — daily aggregate only.
